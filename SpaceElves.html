Literature Review

With respect to user services, solution modeling, and data synchronization, we found five appropriate kinds of literature all written by doctors or professors from the university, through the search engine of 2D Search. We deliberately select articles with a variety of both qualitative and quantitative research from reputable journals whose work was cited in a sufficient amount of other academic works. All the articles were published between 2010 and 2020 ensuring their recency reflecting their relevance and efficacy.

  • Physical Activity of Office Workers

    Rapid progress of civilization almost completely deprives of the opportunity to movement and physical effort. It eliminates from the everyday work all these forms based on physical activity and simple physical effort, leaving mainly monotonous activities that unevenly load the individual parts and systems of the human body.

    Biernat, Tomaszewski and Milde, 2010 Physical Activity of Office Workers

  • Gamification in sport apps

    Gamification is a tool with great potential to motivate individuals to increase their physical activity.

    Bitrián, Buil and Catalán, 2020 Gamification in sport apps: the determinants of users' motivation

  • The relationship between personality traits and susceptibility to social influence

    Research has shown that social influence can be leveraged as a persuasive strategy to elicit beneficial behaviors, especially if it is tailored to the target audience.

    Oyibo and Vassileva, 2019 The relationship between personality traits and susceptibility to social influence

  • Does Social User Experience Improve Motivation for Runners?

    In efforts to enhance the user experience (UX), mobile fitness applications are beginning to incorporate gameplay mechanics and social elements in their design. Unlike the more traditional health applications, m-health applications can provide a richer social user experience that caters to mobile usage contexts, such as fitness.

    Spillers and Asimakopoulos, 2014 Does Social User Experience Improve Motivation for Runners?

Field Research

Geographic and Behavioural Observations

GPS tracking reveals that people tend to run for closure trails. They complete closures with the origin as the starting point. Distances range from 3km-5km. Their choices of running places are most likely along riversides. 4 normally choose leisure places (e.g. parks); 1 run randomly on street; 1 run on playground.

Develop

-Persona-

The preliminary investigations help us sum up two representative personas: Jarrod and Teresa. Jarrod represents the group type of being extroverted, analytical, sensible, loyal to plans, and self-activated. He states that the goal for engaging in running is to stay in shape and gain emotional relaxation off work--although he is able to run on a regular basis, sometimes there could be a hard time running without proper guidance. The Space elves game gives the entertaining elements and guidance through running.

Teresa represents the group type of rather introverted, creative, loyal to plans. Compared to Jarrod, she is not that same self-motivated at running and complains children keep her busy so that she may forget running at weekends. However, she feels regular running is an ideal habit since it keeps her energetic. Space elves' game helps her enhance her passion by splitting persistent running goals into smaller tasks. User journey map also visualizes the two users’ life conditions at different stages--before, during, and after running.

-Main Functions-

VR Game

Run! Space Elves is a game that combines AR technology and running. Players collect a variety of different space pets through sports to become an excellent trainer. Players obtain Space energy and seeds by completing daily sports goals, and use them to incubate and evolve their own unique Space pets. The Space monsters and plants set in the game can be seen in real time through AR, which gives players more fun and motivation to run with.

Test

-Tree Test-

It is a method for evaluating the proposed site structure by asking users to find items based on-site organisation and terminology. We recruited 10 testers as our target users to conduct a tree test on the running application. All the 10 participants had previous work experience and had recently participated in outdoor running. The details of participation are shown in Table 2. This test was conducted remotely through the website of OptimalWorkshop, and for the tree test task we set up three task flows in three different states of running. See Table 3 for specific tasks.

-Usability Test-

This method of evaluating user interfaces involves using a combination of methods to collect data in a controlled environment. The primary goal is to determine whether a project can be used by the intended user group to perform the tasks for which it was designed. This project was based on a prototype we designed about running for participants to test. In order to ensure the validity of the test results, we made the testing process all live and collaborative, so we developed a detailed usability testing scenario of 18 task scenarios, see Table 4. As the testing content of the entire project was expected to be evaluated in the field, we selected two participations out of the 10 remote testers to be our usability testers.
